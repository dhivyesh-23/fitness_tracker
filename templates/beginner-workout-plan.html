BEGINNER WEEKLY WORKOUT PLAN

A gentle introduction to fitness for those new to exercise

Welcome to your beginner workout plan! This program is designed for those who are new to regular exercise. You'll work out 3 days per week with plenty of rest in between. Focus on learning proper form, consistency, and gradually building strength and endurance.

Important Notes:

  • Always begin with a 5-minute warm-up (light walking, marching in place, or gentle stretching)
  • Start with lighter weights than you think you need
  • Rest 60-90 seconds between sets
  • If you feel pain (not to be confused with muscle fatigue), stop the exercise
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout
  • End each session with 5-10 minutes of gentle stretching

MONDAY: FULL BODY BASICS

1. Bodyweight Squats
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your body as if sitting in a chair, keeping your chest up. Push through your heels to return to standing.

2. Wall Push-Ups
Sets: 2 | Reps: 8-10

Stand about arm's length from a wall. Place your hands on the wall at shoulder height. Bend your elbows to bring your chest toward the wall, then push back to the starting position.

3. Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
Sets: 2 | Reps: 8

Sit on a chair with back support. Hold light dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward. Press the weights overhead until arms are extended, then lower back down.

4. Standing Bicep Curls
Sets: 2 | Reps: 8

Stand with arms at sides holding light dumbbells, palms facing forward. Keeping upper arms stationary, curl the weights toward your shoulders, then lower.

5. Marching in Place
Duration: 2 minutes

March in place, lifting knees to hip height if possible. Swing arms naturally to increase intensity.

TUESDAY: REST DAY

Take today to rest and recover. Light activity like walking or gentle stretching is encouraged but not required. Make sure to stay hydrated and get plenty of sleep.

WEDNESDAY: CARDIO & CORE

1. Brisk Walking
Duration: 15 minutes

Walk at a pace where you can still hold a conversation but feel your heart rate increase. You can split this into 3 sets of 5 minutes if needed.

2. Modified Plank
Sets: 2 | Duration: 15-20 seconds

Start on hands and knees. Walk hands forward and drop to forearms. Keep knees on the ground and form a straight line from knees to head. Hold the position.

3. Seated Knee Lifts
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10 (each side)

Sit on the edge of a chair with good posture. Lift one knee up toward your chest, then lower it back down. Alternate sides.

4. Seated Leg Extensions
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10 (each leg)

Sit on a chair with good posture. Extend one leg straight out, hold for 2 seconds, then lower. Repeat with the other leg.

5. Standing Side Bends
Sets: 2 | Reps: 8 (each side)

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Place one hand on your hip and extend the other overhead. Bend sideways toward the hand on your hip, then return to center. Repeat on the other side.

THURSDAY: REST DAY

Take today to rest and recover. Light activity like walking or gentle stretching is encouraged but not required. Make sure to stay hydrated and get plenty of sleep.

FRIDAY: STRENGTH & MOBILITY

1. Chair Assisted Squats
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10

Place a chair behind you. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower down as if sitting in the chair (touch lightly or sit fully if needed), then push through heels to stand back up.

2. Countertop Push-Ups
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10

Place hands on a countertop or sturdy table, slightly wider than shoulder-width. Step feet back so your body forms a straight line. Bend elbows to lower chest toward the counter, then push back up.

3. Seated Rows with Resistance Band
Sets: 2 | Reps: 10

Sit on a chair with legs extended. Loop a light resistance band around your feet and hold ends with both hands. Pull the band toward your lower ribs, squeezing shoulder blades together, then slowly release.

4. Standing Calf Raises
Sets: 2 | Reps: 12

Stand with feet hip-width apart, holding onto a wall or chair for balance. Rise up onto the balls of your feet, hold briefly at the top, then lower back down.

5. Wall Angels
Sets: 2 | Reps: 8

Stand with your back against a wall, feet hip-width apart. Press your lower back, shoulders, and head against the wall. With elbows bent at 90 degrees, place arms against the wall in a "W" position, then slide arms up and down like making a snow angel.

SATURDAY & SUNDAY: ACTIVE RECOVERY

Take the weekend to rest, but try to include some light activity such as:

  • A 15-20 minute leisurely walk
  • Gentle stretching for 10 minutes
  • Light household chores
  • Gardening

The goal is to keep moving without taxing your muscles too much as they recover from the week's workouts.
