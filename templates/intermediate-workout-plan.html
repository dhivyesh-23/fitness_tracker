INTERMEDIATE WEEKLY WORKOUT PLAN

A progressive program for those with basic fitness experience

Welcome to your intermediate workout plan! This program is designed for those who have been exercising consistently for at least 2-3 months and are comfortable with basic movement patterns. This plan introduces more challenging exercises, higher volume, and incorporates supersets to increase intensity.

Important Notes:

  • Always begin with a 5-8 minute dynamic warm-up
  • Rest 45-60 seconds between sets (unless specified in supersets)
  • Choose weights that make the last 2 reps challenging but doable with good form
  • Track your weights to ensure progressive overload
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout
  • End each session with 5-10 minutes of stretching

MONDAY: PUSH DAY (CHEST, SHOULDERS, TRICEPS)

1. Push-Ups
Sets: 3 | Reps: 10-12

Start in a high plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor, keeping elbows at a 45-degree angle from your body. Push back up to starting position.

Superset A (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)

2a. Dumbbell Bench Press
Sets: 3 | Reps: 10

Lie on a flat bench holding dumbbells at chest level with palms facing forward. Press weights up until arms are extended, then lower them back down with control.

2b. Dumbbell Lateral Raises
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart holding light dumbbells at your sides. With a slight bend in your elbows, raise arms out to the sides until they're parallel with the floor, then lower with control.

Superset B (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)

3a. Romanian Deadlifts
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Stand holding dumbbells in front of your thighs with palms facing your body. With knees slightly bent, hinge at the hips and lower the weights while keeping your back flat. Feel a stretch in your hamstrings, then return to standing by driving your hips forward.

3b. Side Planks
Sets: 3 | Duration: 20-30 seconds (each side)

Lie on your side with legs extended and feet stacked. Prop yourself up on your forearm with elbow under shoulder. Raise hips to create a straight line from head to feet. Hold, then switch sides.

4. Glute Bridges with Dumbbell
Sets: 3 | Reps: 15

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Place a dumbbell on your hips (optional). Press through your heels to lift your hips toward the ceiling, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower back down with control.

SATURDAY: UPPER BODY & HIIT

1. Push-Up Variations (choose based on ability)
Sets: 3 | Reps: 10-12

Options: Standard push-ups, incline push-ups (hands on elevated surface), or diamond push-ups (hands close together under chest). Perform with proper form until muscles are challenged.

Circuit Training (perform each exercise for 40 seconds, rest 20 seconds, complete 3-4 rounds)

2a. Dumbbell Renegade Rows

Start in a push-up position with hands on dumbbells. Perform a row by lifting one dumbbell to ribcage while balancing on the other arm, alternate sides.

2b. Overhead Tricep Extensions

Stand holding a dumbbell with both hands above your head, elbows pointing forward. Lower the weight behind your head by bending at the elbows, then extend back up.

2c. Alternating Bicep Curls

Stand holding dumbbells at your sides. Curl one dumbbell toward your shoulder while rotating your palm upward, lower and repeat with other arm.

2d. Dumbbell Chest Flyes

Lie on your back holding dumbbells above your chest with palms facing each other. With slight bend in elbows, lower weights out to sides in an arc motion, then bring back together above chest.

3. HIIT Finisher (4 rounds)
  • 30 seconds Burpees
  • 30 seconds Rest

SUNDAY: REST DAY

Take today as a complete rest day to allow your body to recover. Focus on:

  • Proper nutrition for muscle recovery
  • Staying well-hydrated
  • Getting adequate sleep
  • Light stretching if desired

Use this day to prepare mentally and physically for the upcoming week of training.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10

Sit on a bench with back support. Hold dumbbells at shoulder height with palms facing forward. Press weights overhead until arms are fully extended, then lower back to starting position.

3b. Tricep Dips (bench)
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Sit on the edge of a bench, hands gripping the edge beside your hips. Slide your butt off the bench, supporting your weight with your hands. Lower your body by bending your elbows until they reach about 90 degrees, then press back up.

4. Incline Dumbbell Flyes
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Lie on an incline bench holding dumbbells above your chest with palms facing each other. With a slight bend in your elbows, lower the weights in an arc to the sides until you feel a stretch in your chest, then bring them back up.

TUESDAY: CARDIO & CORE

1. HIIT Cardio Circuit (repeat 3-4 times with 90 seconds rest between circuits)
  • 30 seconds Jumping Jacks
  • 30 seconds High Knees
  • 30 seconds Mountain Climbers
  • 30 seconds Bodyweight Squats

Core Circuit (perform each exercise with minimal rest, rest 60 seconds between circuits, repeat 3 times)

2a. Plank
Duration: 30-45 seconds

Support your weight on forearms and toes, maintaining a straight line from head to heels. Keep core tight and don't let hips sag.

2b. Russian Twists
Reps: 20 (10 each side)

Sit on the floor with knees bent and feet elevated slightly. Lean back to create a V-shape with your torso and thighs. Clasp hands together and twist your torso to tap the floor on each side.

2c. Bicycle Crunches
Reps: 20 (10 each side)

Lie on your back with hands behind head and legs lifted. Bring one knee toward your chest while twisting to touch it with the opposite elbow. Extend that leg while bringing the other knee in and twisting to the other side.

2d. Glute Bridges
Reps: 15

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Press through your heels to lift your hips toward the ceiling, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower back down with control.

WEDNESDAY: PULL DAY (BACK & BICEPS)

1. Dumbbell Rows
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12 (each side)

Place one knee and hand on a bench, with opposite foot on floor. Hold a dumbbell in free hand, arm extended. Pull the dumbbell up to your ribcage, squeezing your shoulder blade, then lower with control.

Superset A (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)

2a. Lat Pulldowns (with resistance band or machine)
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Secure a resistance band above you or use a lat pulldown machine. Grasp the band/bar with hands wider than shoulder-width. Pull down toward your upper chest, squeezing shoulder blades together, then return with control.

2b. Dumbbell Bicep Curls
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding dumbbells with arms at sides and palms facing forward. Keeping upper arms stationary, curl weights toward shoulders, then lower with control.

Superset B (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)

3a. Bent-Over Reverse Flyes
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Bend at the hips with a flat back, holding light dumbbells with palms facing each other. Keeping a slight bend in elbows, raise arms out to the sides until parallel with the floor, squeezing shoulder blades together. Lower with control.

3b. Hammer Curls
Sets: 3 | Reps: 12

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding dumbbells with arms at sides and palms facing your body. Keeping upper arms stationary, curl weights toward shoulders while maintaining neutral grip, then lower with control.

4. Superman Hold
Sets: 3 | Duration: 20 seconds

Lie face down with arms extended overhead. Simultaneously lift your arms, chest, and legs off the floor, holding this position while squeezing your lower back.

5. Concentration Curls
Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 (each arm)

Sit on a bench with legs spread, holding a dumbbell in one hand. Rest your elbow against your inner thigh. Curl the weight up toward your shoulder, focusing on the contraction in your bicep, then lower with control.

THURSDAY: REST DAY OR ACTIVE RECOVERY

Take today to rest and recover. Consider these light activities to promote recovery:

  • 20-30 minute walk
  • 15-20 minutes of yoga
  • Light mobility work
  • Foam rolling

Focus on hydration and nutrition today to support muscle recovery.

FRIDAY: LEGS & CORE

1. Goblet Squats
Sets: 4 | Reps: 12

Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell close to your chest with both hands. Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower into a squat, keeping chest up and knees tracking over toes, then push through heels to return to standing.

Superset A (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)

2a. Walking Lunges
Sets: 3 | Reps: 20 (10 each leg)

Hold dumbbells at your sides. Step forward with one leg, lowering until both knees form 90-degree angles. Push through front heel to bring back leg forward into the next lunge step.

2b. Calf Raises
Sets: 3 | Reps: 15

Stand with feet hip-width apart, holding dumbbells at your sides or on a raised platform for greater range of motion. Rise up onto the balls of your feet, hold briefly at the top, then lower with control.

Superset B (complete both exercises before resting 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds)
